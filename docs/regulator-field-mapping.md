# Regulator field mapping

PRAMAN emits one append-only audit record per claim. Each record is a structured, tamper-evident artifact that a deployer can hand to a regulator or auditor as evidence that AI-generated output was checked against its evidence, that the residual risk was bounded and documented, and that the decision is traceable to a specific model and calibration version. This document maps each field of that record to the concrete obligation it helps satisfy under three frameworks: the EU AI Act Article 50 (transparency and disclosure of AI-generated or potentially inaccurate output), the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (primarily the Measure function, which identifies, measures, and tracks AI risk, with touchpoints into Map and Manage), and the HIPAA Security Rule AI risk-analysis update (February 2026), which requires a documented risk analysis of AI systems that touch electronic protected health information (ePHI).

The audit record schema is: `ts`, `claim`, `evidence_span`, `p_grounded`, `decision`, `policy` (with sub-keys `alpha`, `delta`, `class`, `method`), `model_version`, `calib_version`, and `content_hash`.

## Field-by-field mapping

| Audit field | EU AI Act Article 50 (transparency) | NIST AI RMF (Measure, with Map/Manage) | HIPAA Security Rule AI risk-analysis (Feb 2026) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `ts` (timestamp) | Establishes when the disclosure-relevant check occurred, so an inaccurate-output determination can be tied to a point in time. | Measure: timestamps make risk measurements trackable over time and support drift and trend monitoring. | Supports the dated, ongoing nature of a documented risk analysis covering ePHI processing events. |
| `claim` (the atomic claim checked) | Identifies the specific AI-generated statement that is subject to transparency, rather than disclosing the whole output opaquely. | Measure: defines the unit of analysis at which groundedness risk is identified and measured. | Pinpoints the specific generated assertion that may touch ePHI, so the risk analysis is concrete, not generic. |
| `evidence_span` (the supporting evidence) | Records the basis against which accuracy was assessed, supporting a defensible disclosure of how output was validated. | Map/Measure: documents the evidence context (Map) and the reference against which support is measured (Measure). | Documents the source material the claim was checked against, evidence that ePHI-related output was validated against grounding. |
| `p_grounded` (calibrated probability) | Provides a calibrated, quantified confidence behind any accuracy disclosure, not an opaque or heuristic score. | Measure: the core quantitative risk measurement per claim, calibrated so the number is meaningful. | Quantifies residual likelihood that a claim is ungrounded, a measured risk input the analysis must record. |
| `decision` (accept / reject / escalate) | Shows the action taken on potentially inaccurate output (auto-clear, block, or route to human), making the transparency outcome auditable. | Manage: records the risk-response decision (accept, reject, or escalate to a human), closing the measure-to-manage loop. | Demonstrates the safeguard applied to ePHI-touching output, including human escalation, as a documented risk control. |
| `policy.alpha` (target risk level α) | Documents the explicit bound on auto-approving an ungrounded claim that governs the disclosure threshold. | Measure: states the quantitative risk tolerance against which realized risk is measured and tracked. | Records the chosen acceptable-risk level for ePHI-related auto-approval, the analysis's documented risk threshold. |
| `policy.delta` (confidence 1 - δ) | Documents the statistical confidence attached to the bound, so the disclosure's reliability is itself stated. | Measure: captures the confidence level of the risk measurement, distinguishing a bound from a point estimate. | Records the confidence with which the risk bound holds, part of a rigorous, defensible risk-analysis posture. |
| `policy.class` (decision class) | Identifies which category of output the transparency treatment applies to, supporting context-appropriate disclosure. | Map: situates the claim in its deployment context and use-case category, where risk is framed before it is measured. | Identifies the ePHI-relevant decision category (for example clinical), so the risk analysis is scoped to context. |
| `policy.method` (risk-control method) | Names the documented mechanism (for example CRC) behind the accuracy determination, supporting a defensible disclosure. | Measure: records the measurement methodology, a prerequisite for reproducible and trackable risk metrics. | Documents the specific risk-control methodology applied, supporting the "how risk was analyzed" element of the analysis. |
| `model_version` | Ties the disclosure to the exact verifier that produced it, so accuracy claims are reproducible and attributable. | Measure/Manage: enables traceability of measurements to a versioned system and supports change management. | Establishes which AI version processed ePHI-related content, required for an auditable, version-specific risk analysis. |
| `calib_version` | Ties the disclosure to the calibration run behind the probability and thresholds, so the stated accuracy is reproducible. | Measure: records the provenance of the calibration that makes the risk numbers valid and reproducible. | Documents the calibration basis of the risk measurement, evidence the residual-risk figures for ePHI are grounded in a known dataset. |
| `content_hash` | Provides tamper-evidence that the disclosed claim-and-evidence pair was not altered after the fact. | Measure/Manage: integrity of the recorded measurement supports a trustworthy, tamper-evident risk log. | Supports the integrity and auditability of the risk-analysis record for ePHI-related content (a hash, not the raw PHI). |

## Closing note

This mapping is an evidence-mapping aid intended to help a deployer connect PRAMAN's audit output to the documentation each framework expects. It is not legal advice and not a certification of compliance. Whether any specific deployment satisfies the EU AI Act, the NIST AI RMF, or the HIPAA Security Rule depends on the full system, its data handling, and the deployer's own controls, and should be confirmed with qualified counsel.
